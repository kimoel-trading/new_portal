REVISED COLLEGE ADMISSION POLICY

I. RATIONALE

Batangas State University, The National Engineering University (BatStateU The NEU) maintains its commitment to provide quality, excellent, relevant and responsive education by providing testing and admission services, and to ensure students’ academic success in preparation for a globally competitive workforce.

The Revised College Admission policy, hereinafter referred to “Policy”, is anchored on Republic Act (RA) No. 10931 or the Universal Access to Quality Tertiary Education Act. The said piece of landmark legislation provides for the protection and promotion of the rights of all students to quality education at all levels. This aligns with the United Nation’s call for Sustainable Development Goals specifically, SDG 4 Quality Education, “ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong opportunities for all,” and SDG 10 Reduced Inequalities, “reduce inequality within and among countries.”

As the University progresses to keep its commitment to global sustainability, it also extends its efforts to democratize access to college education through the Affirmative Action Program (AAP) to ensure that all students have continuous access to educational opportunities which contributes to reducing inequalities. Moreover, it responds to Chapter 2, Section 12 of RA No. 7277, as amended, or the Magna Carta for Disabled Persons, the University shall, “ensure that disabled persons are provided with adequate access to quality education and ample opportunities to develop their skill.” True to the law’s mandates, the University shall provide reasonable accommodations to ensure that students with disabilities can fully participate in academic programs.

II. SCOPE

Section 1. This Policy shall apply to all applicants for admission to undergraduate academic programs offered by the University in all its campuses, colleges, and departments.

III. DEFINITION OF TERMS

Section 2. For the purposes of this Policy, the following terms shall mean:

  • a. Academic Strands – refer to STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics); ABM (Accountancy, Business, and Management); HUMSS (Humanities and Social Sciences); GAS (General Academic Strand), PBM (Pre-Baccalaureate Maritime); TVL (Technical Vocational Livelihood)
  • b. Admission – refers to the process through which students enter the undergraduate program.
  • c. Admission Test – refers to a comprehensive, multidimensional battery test which is used as an aid for selection & classification.
  • d. Affirmative Action Program – refers to admission of indigent applicants, ALS graduates, students with special needs, PWD, children of solo parents, those belonging to the indigenous people/communities and Iskolar ng Bayan, to programs in the University.
  • e. ALS graduates – refer to students who completed the Alternative Learning System (ALS) in accordance with the rules promulgated by the Department of Education (DepEd).
  • f. Children of Solo Parents – refer to the children whose parents are raising a child or children on their own without the support of a partner or spouse; or any individual who falls under any of the categories mentioned under RA 8972 or the Solo Parents’ Welfare Act of 2000.
  • g. Final Grade – refers to a mark or grade based on the weighted raw score of the learners’ summative assessment as indicated in the K to 12 Basic Education Program which uses a standards- and competency-based grading system in Grades 10 and 11.
  • h. Indigent applicants – refer to student applicants from low-income families with a yearly income of Four Hundred Thousand Pesos (PhP400,000) or less.
  • i. Indigenous Cultural Communities/Indigenous people – refer to a group of people or homogenous societies identified by self-ascription and ascription by others, who have continuously lived as organized community on communally bounded and defined territory, and who have, under claims of ownership since time immemorial, occupied, possessed and utilized such territories, sharing common bonds of language, customs, traditions and other distinctive cultural traits or who have, through resistance to political, social and cultural inroads of colonization, non-indigenous religions and cultures, became historically differentiated from the majority of Filipinos. ICCs/IPs shall likewise include peoples who are regarded as indigenous on account of their descent from the populations which inhabited the country, at the time of conquest or colonization, or at the time of inroads of non-indigenous religions and cultures, or the establishment of present state boundaries, who retain some or all of their own social, economic, cultural and political institutions, but who may have been displaced from their traditional domains or who may have resettled outside their ancestral domains in accordance with RA 8371 or The Indigenous Peoples Rights Act of 1997.
  • j. Iskolar ng Bayan Program – refers to the program that any public high school student who belongs to the top ten (10) bracket of the graduating class shall be entitled of automatic admission in any State Universities and Colleges (SUC) of choice upon compliance with the requirements under RA No. 10648 or the Iskolar ng Bayan Program Act of 2014.
  • k. Persons with Disabilities (PWDs) – refer to individuals who have long term physical, mental, intellectual, or sensory impairments which in interaction with various barriers may hinder their full and effective participation in society in equal basis with others, that can be broadly categorized into several types, including but not limited to: Physical Disabilities, Learning Disabilities, Cognitive Disabilities, Psychiatric Disabilities, Sensory Disabilities, Speech and Language Disabilities and Other Disabilities as mentioned in RA No. 7277, as amended.

IV. ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENT FOR THE ADMISSION OF INCOMING FIRST YEAR COLLEGE STUDENT

Section 3. Applicants for admission to all college programs of the University shall be required to take the Batangas State University College Admission Test (BatStateUCAT).

A. GENERAL REQUIREMENTS FOR BATSTATEU CAT

Section 4. Applicants must have completed high school in any recognized school or must be enrolled in Senior High School, if not yet completed.

Section 5. Applicants must submit the following:

  1. E-copy of Filled-out Application Form
  2. E-copy of one (1) recent Passport size picture with white or plain background
  3. Certified e-copy of final grades in Grades 10, and 11 in Math, English and Science
    • Grades Form 1 (for Regular Admission) / Grades Form 2 (for ALS)
    • Junior High School and Senior High School Form 137
    • Certification of completion of SHS and respective strand or enrolment therein, if not completed

The original hard copy of requirements shall be submitted upon request of the Testing and Admission Office (TAO), the University Registrar, and/or the College concerned.

D. EVALUATION OF APPLICATIONS AND CRITERIA FOR ADMISSION PRIORITY

Section 8. Applicants shall be evaluated and ranked in accordance with the BatStateUCAT score (60%) and (40%) of the average in Math, Science and English for two years preceding graduation. Applicants are admitted to programs applied for based on rank subject to quota set or slots allotted per program by the University.

Section 9. Applicants shall be given three (3) program choices they are interested in. If an applicant is not successful for their first choice, the process continues in the order of priority of the remaining choices. Applicants who have not been selected for any of their chosen programs will be considered to programs with available slots.

Section 10. Applicants shall seek admission to the Campus nearest their place of residence where the program applied for is offered. The University shall allow admitted students for accommodation in any other campus when programs are not available therein, as practicable.

Section 11. Applicants from different strand/ track may be admitted to program applied for, after the admission of the qualified applicants with aligned track / strand, based on rank subject to available slots allotted per program by the University.

Section 12. Applicants from BatStateU The NEU Integrated School (IS) and Laboratory School (LS) shall be exempted in taking the BatStateU CAT and shall be automatically admitted to the STEM program of choice based on rank on their Final Grades in Math, Science and English. Applicants who do not meet the grade requirements will be reconsidered for non-board STEM programs.

Section 13. The automatic admission of Filipino public high school graduates who are in the Top Ten of their graduating class shall be implemented in the University in accordance with RA No. 10648, provided that all other University admission requirements are met.

Section 14. Indigent applicants, ALS graduates, students with special needs (SSN), persons with disabilities (PWD), Iskolar ng Bayan, children of solo parents, and those belonging to the ICC/IPs shall be admitted to programs of the University. The University shall allot twenty percent (20%) of the total slots or enrolments in programs to accommodate admissions of these applicants. The total number of slots for AAP may be increased based on the average recorded number of qualified AAP applicants and average total population of students in the University in the last four (4) academic years, and capacity of programs at the start of the current academic year. Potential for success in the program of each applicant shall be evaluated.

E. AFFIRMATIVE ACTION PROGRAM

Section 15. Requirements for Admission under Affirmative Action Program (AAP)

Applicants under AAP must submit the following requirements:

  • Indigent Students - Certification of indigency from their respective barangay of parent or guardian, if available and Certificate of Tax Exemption from the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR).
  • ALS Graduates - Certification of completion of ALS program, or enrolment therein, if not completed.
  • Indigenous People - Certificate of Tribe Membership, or any equivalent certification as belonging to indigenous people/indigenous cultural community.
  • Persons With Disability - PWD Identification Card
  • Iskolar ng Bayan - Certification from the public high school, duly signed by the school principal, of the rank of the applicant in the Top Ten of the graduating (Grade 12) class.
  • Children of Solo Parents - Solo parent ID

Section 16. Procedures for Indigent Applicants under Programmed Admission shall be the same with other applicants.

F. NON-DISCRIMINATION POLICY

Section 17. The University shall provide a 21st century learning environment through innovations in education, multidisciplinary research, and community and industry partnerships in order to nurture the spirit of nationhood, propel the national economy, and engage the world for sustainable development. As a matter of policy, all qualified applicants regardless of race, religion, sexual orientation, gender identity, gender expression, disability or national origin or any other category protected by applicable law shall not be discriminated against in all the procedures provided in all university admission policies. This also applies to all rights and privileges, programs, and activities generally accorded or made available to students of the University.

G. CONDUCT OF BATSTATEU CAT

Section 18. BatStateU CAT shall be conducted as part of the ranking process as the University has to limit slots for college admission to each campus and to its various degree programs.

H. INTEGRITY OF APPLICATION

Section 19. The applicant guarantees the completeness and accuracy of information disclosed or contained in documents submitted. Any form of misrepresentation, falsification, and any form of dishonesty committed in relation to application for admission shall be sufficient ground for disqualification from admission to University for a specified period or permanently depending on the severity of the violation and as may be determined by the Appeals Committee.

V. IMPLEMENTATION, MONITORING AND EVALUATION

Section 20. The Office of the Vice President for Academic Affairs, Constituent Campuses, Colleges, and Testing and Admission Office shall implement, monitor and gather feedback on the implementation of this Policy, and recommend provisions for revision and/or amendments.

VI. APPLICABILITY OF THE DATA PRIVACY MANUAL

Section 21. The provisions of this Policy shall be applied in harmony with the University Data Privacy Manual, especially on the technical and physical measures on data collection, use, storage, retention and disposal. In case of irreconcilable conflict between this Policy and the University Data Privacy Manual, the matter should be referred to the President’s Advisory Council (PAC).

VII. EFFECTIVITY

Section 22. This Policy shall take effect upon approval by the Board of Regents. Any provision hereof that may be in conflict with any existing laws, rules or regulations promulgated by higher authorities shall be deemed inoperative.
