Definition of Terms

Internally Displaced Persons People or groups of people who have been forced or obliged to flee or to leave their homes due to armed conflict, or to avoid the effects of armed conflict, situations of generalized violence, violations of human rights, or natural or human-made disasters and who have not crossed an international border. (e.g. residence relocated due to disaster such as typhoon, earthquake or volcanic eruption).
Persons With Disability Are persons who have long-term physical, mental, intellectual or sensory impairments which is an interaction with various barriers may hinder their full and effective participation in society on an equal basis with others.
Psychiatric Disorder A disorder characterized by a clinically significant disturbance in an individual's cognition, emotional regulation, or behaviour (e.g. anxiety disorder, depression, bipolar disorder, schizophrenia, etc.)
Disability caused by chronic illness A disability that results from complications brought about by the worsening of a chronic medical condition. (e.g. diabetes, cancer, kidney-failure, etc.)
Learning Disability A disability that makes it difficult for a person to focus, comprehend, and retain information.
Visual Disability Visual disability is considered a disability only when it can no longer be corrected or as deemed by the attending.
Orthopedic/Locomotor Disability A disability that interferes with the ability of the joints, muscles, or limbs to function normally. (e.g. polio, cerebral palsy)
Communication Disability A disability with two sub-types: hearing impairment and speech and language impairment. A person is said to be hearing impaired when he or she partially or completely lose his or her hearing ability. Meanwhile, a person with a speech and language impairment has trouble articulating words. (deaf and/or mute)
Congenital Disease A structural or functional anomalies that is present at birth which may have hereditary or non-hereditary cause.
